<objective>
Ship the LangGraph agent that turns a user question into a cited answer (or an honest refusal) end-to-end. This is the Walking Skeleton (per SKELETON.md): no UI, no deploy, no Langfuse — but the contracts (`GroundedAnswer`, `cite_check`, three refusal branches) are all live and exercised by `python -m agent.demo`. The deliverable proves the architecture works before Wave 4 invests in Streamlit polish.

Purpose: This plan closes the four most load-bearing Phase 1 REQs — RAG-01 (plain-English questions answered grounded), RAG-02 (every claim has a span-level citation), RAG-03 (refusal posture for ungrounded queries), TRUST-04 (non-LLM cite-check gates every answer). The deterministic cite-check algorithm (PITFALL P5 antibody) and the dual-gate refusal posture (D-05) are the project's honesty-first credibility surface — get them right here and every later phase inherits them; get them wrong and the entire portfolio piece's narrative collapses on one bad citation.

Type `tdd`: every node's behavior is testable in isolation against fixture chunks; the integration tests in `tests/integration/test_agent_e2e.py` and `tests/integration/test_drhp_prompt_injection.py` exercise the compiled graph against the real Qdrant collection populated by Wave 2.

- A synthetic 10-chunk fixture (`tests/fixtures/synthetic_chunks.jsonl`) for unit tests that don't hit real Qdrant
